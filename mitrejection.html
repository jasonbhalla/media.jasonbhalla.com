December 17, 2022

Dear Jason,

On behalf of the Admissions Committee, it is my pleasure to offer you admission to the MIT Class of 2027!

Just kidding. LOL. This year’s Early Action pool was one of the largest in MIT’s history, and yet you single-handedly stood out as one of the worst applicants that we've ever seen. We had thought that all of our applicants were exceptional students, and then we read your application.

Did you seriously think you had a chance? Your response to the essay prompt, "Tell us about something you do simply for the pleasure of it", was "Being cool". This is not up to the standards that we're looking for.

You didn't even answer the other essay prompts. You just wrote, "This is Joe Biden on Jasons computer. I can ensure that jason is the best student to ever apply for MIT!" You may have fooled yourself into believing your own delusions, but you didn't fool us.

It really isn't a good look when an applicant downright bribes our admissions officers with Bitcoin in return for acceptance. Furthermore, we know that Abraham Lincoln didn't write the Letter of Recommendation you submitted. How did we know this? Because he's DEAD, like you should be.

The Admissions Committee is personally drafting a Letter of De-Recommendation on your behalf. We're going to submit it to every other college you apply to.

We wish you the worst in all of your future endeavors.


Sincerely,

Tim Ekaf
Dean of Admissions and Student Financial Services
